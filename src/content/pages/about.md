## Software?

I started programming because I wanted to understand how computers actually work. The ability to instruct silicon to coordinate data remains deeply fascinating to me. I believe that software engineering is a craft requiring patience, humility, and continuous study.

## Why Cybersecurity?

Building software requires understanding boundaries. Studying security forces you to inspect how protocols behave under invalid configurations or malicious loads. By studying cybersecurity, I learn to design code that is secure by default and resilient against failures.

## Why Writing?

I do not write because I have mastered systems. I write because it is the process through which I analyze and understand them. If I cannot write a simple explanation of an architecture, I do not understand the system.

## Why Build in Public?

Knowing that other engineers might read your notes forces absolute precision. Documenting mistakes, architecture choices, and lessons openly creates a feedback loop that refines my development habits.

## The Kind of Engineer I Want to Become

I aim to become a craftsman-style systems engineer. I value boring, reliable, and clean software architectures that still make sense ten years later. I prefer to reduce systems to their absolute essentials to ensure they are easy to maintain and scale.
